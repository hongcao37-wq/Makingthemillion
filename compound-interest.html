What Is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal and on the accumulated interest from previous periods. In simple terms: your money earns money, and then that money earns even more money.

"Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it. He who doesn't, pays it." — Albert Einstein (attributed)

Simple vs. Compound Interest: The Shocking Difference

Let’s say you invest $10,000 at an 8% annual return.

Year Simple Interest (8% on original) Compound Interest (8% compounded annually)
10 years$18,000$21,589
20 years$26,000$46,610
30 years$34,000$100,627
40 years$42,000$217,245

After 40 years, compound interest turns your $10,000 into over $217,000 — more than 5 times the result of simple interest.

The Real Magic: Time + Consistency

The two most powerful ingredients are:

  1. Time – The earlier you start, the more dramatic the results.
  2. Consistency – Regular contributions supercharge growth.

Example: Investing $500/month at 8% average annual return:

  • After 20 years → ~$294,000 (you invested $120,000)
  • After 30 years → ~$680,000 (you invested $180,000)
  • After 40 years → ~$1,553,000 (you invested $240,000)
Starting at age 25 vs. age 35 can mean hundreds of thousands more by retirement — even with the same monthly amount.

How to Harness Compound Interest Today

  1. Start investing as early as possible.
  2. Choose investments with solid long-term returns (e.g., broad stock market index funds).
  3. Reinvest all dividends and gains.
  4. Automate monthly contributions — never miss a month.
  5. Be patient. The real growth happens in the later years.

Final Thought

Compound interest doesn’t require you to be rich to start — it requires you to start. Even small amounts, invested consistently over decades, can grow into life-changing wealth.

The million isn’t made overnight. It’s built, quietly and powerfully, one compounded dollar at a time.
